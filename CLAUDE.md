## What This Project Is

A website rebuild for **Savannah Personal Care Services (SPCS)**, a home care agency in Savannah, Georgia founded by **Shannon Stafford Simpson**, a Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA).

Two advertising frameworks govern every word and every design decision:

**Claude Hopkins — Scientific Advertising (1923)**
- Specificity over vagueness. Every claim must be verifiable.
- Reason-why copy. Don't describe services — explain why each one exists.
- Preemptive claim. Own what competitors do but haven't said.
- Sampling principle. Give real value before asking for anything.
- The headline selects the right reader. The copy moves them to act.

**WSJ "Tale of Two Young Men" letter structure**
- Open with the reader's situation. Not the product's features.
- Name the exact fear. Dissolve it with specificity.
- Paint the future. Not with hype — with possibility.
- Make the next step feel smaller than the cost of not taking it.
- Never ask for the sale. Let the prospect ask what it costs to continue.

**Eugene Schwartz — Breakthrough Advertising**
- People buy movement. From current state to desired state.
- Sell the bridge. Not the bricks.
- Current state: "I'm running my agency alone."
- Desired state: "I'm the most trusted home care provider in Savannah."
- Copy must create emotional desire. A future picture. A dream. A why.

Home Instead (homeinstead.com) is the industry standard.

### What They Do Right — Incorporate

## Design Direction — Apple-Level Standards

### Core Principles
- **Subtract until only the essential remains.** No decorative elements. No icons performing trust. No gradient cards. No colored card arrays.
- **Typography does the work.** Headline size, weight, and whitespace carry the page. Nothing else should.
- **One bold design choice per section.** Spend boldness once. Keep everything else quiet.
- **Mobile first.** The primary buyer is on her phone at night, in bed, after the kids are asleep.
- **Emotion before information.** The hero must make her feel understood before it tells her anything.

### Hard No List
- No icon grids
- No orange (current site color — avoid entirely)
- No purple or navy (Senior Helpers — differentiate)
- No stock photo carousels
- No chat widgets covering content
- No placeholder text visible anywhere
- No "compassionate" without evidence
- No "tailored solutions" — it means nothing
- No more than one CTA per visible screen

### Type System
- Display: `DM Serif Display` — editorial warmth, not clinical
- Body: `DM Sans` — clean, human, readable on mobile
- Hero headline: 88px desktop / 44px mobile
- Section title: 54px desktop / 32px mobile
- Body: 17px desktop / 15px mobile
- Label: 13px, 500 weight, 0.14em letter-spacing, uppercase
- Letter-spacing on display: `-0.025em`

### Color System
```
--teal:       #0a5c52   primary — CTAs, credential, Shannon's identity
--teal-dark:  #073d36   hover states, footer background
--teal-lt:    #e4f0ee   light teal sections
--sand:       #f5f0e8   hero background, warm sections
--ink:        #1d1d1f   body text (Apple's exact near-black)
--soft:       #6e6e73   secondary text (Apple's secondary gray)
--white:      #ffffff
--rule:       rgba(0,0,0,0.08)
--gold:       #c09040   five stars only — nowhere else
```

### Nav
- Frosted glass: `backdrop-filter: saturate(180%) blur(20px)`
- Height: 52px, fixed
- Logo left · Nav links center (hidden mobile) · Phone number right
- Phone always visible — this is a care business, calls convert

## Voice and Tone

**Write like a trusted friend who happens to know this industry.**

Not clinical. Not a brochure. Not a franchise manual.

Shannon is a CNA who built something real. The copy must sound like someone who has been in the room when families make this decision — and wants to make it less frightening.

- Short sentences carry more weight than long ones
- Specific numbers beat general reassurances
- Name the fear before dissolving it
- Paint the future — not with hype, with possibility
- The reader is intelligent and exhausted. Respect both.
- Hope is the missing ingredient. Every section should leave the reader feeling that things could be better than they are right now — and that calling Shannon is the first step toward that.
